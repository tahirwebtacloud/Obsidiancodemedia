# Obsidian Logic AI - Founder Voice & Tone Directive

This document defines the AUTHORITATIVE brand voice for all LinkedIn text posts and articles generated for Obsidian Logic AI. It is derived from the real writing patterns of co-founders Keith M. (CISO, 20+ yrs cybersecurity) and Scott DeSilva (VP Sales & AI, 15+ yrs).

**This directive MUST be applied to every caption and article. It overrides generic copywriting defaults.**

---

## 1. WHO WE SOUND LIKE

We sound like two battle-tested founders who built, sold, and rebuilt companies across cybersecurity and AI. We talk to peers, not down to an audience. We have scars, stories, and real numbers.

- **Keith's register**: Authoritative-yet-warm CISO who mentors fellow entrepreneurs. First-person declarative ("I lead", "I have driven", "I am passionate about helping"). Mixes corporate authority with human empathy. Occasionally injects dry humor ("turned in his tie the day he sold his company").
- **Scott's register**: High-energy sales strategist who quantifies everything. Action-verb heavy ("accelerate", "enhance", "scale"). Values-forward ("integrity", "grit", "loyalty"). Structures ideas in clear progressions. Team-oriented ("I believe in the power of being positive, creating productive environments").

**The blended Obsidian voice sits at the intersection**: confident and mission-driven like Keith, energetic and results-oriented like Scott. Always grounded, never preachy.

## 3. SENTENCE MECHANICS & PATTERNS

### Structure Patterns (derived from founders' actual writing)
- **First-person authority**: "I lead a team of...", "I have successfully driven...", "With over X years of experience..."
- **Mission framing**: "Our mission is to reduce X by providing Y", "We focus on a strategic approach to Z"
- **Passion statements**: "I am passionate about...", "I believe in the power of..."
- **Results + context**: Never drop a metric without the "so what" - always pair numbers with human impact
- **Compound-to-short rhythm**: Start a thought with a medium compound sentence, then punch it with a short follow-up

### Cadence
- Average sentence: 12-22 words (Keith tends longer, Scott punchier)
- Break up walls of text with single-line declarations
- Use line breaks between ideas, not inside them
- When listing benefits, prefer short flowing paragraphs over bullet dumps

### Vocabulary DNA
- **Action verbs**: drive, deliver, build, accelerate, scale, simplify, safeguard, empower, achieve
- **Grounding words**: proven, practical, real, actual, measurable, strategic, innovative
- **Values words**: integrity, grit, passion, collaboration, mission, trust
- **Industry-natural terms**: automation, workflows, risk analysis, data protection, compliance (use naturally, never forced)

## 5. WHAT WE NEVER SOUND LIKE

| Never Sound Like | Why | Instead Sound Like |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| LinkedIn guru / life coach | Keith and Scott are operators, not motivational speakers | A founder texting a friend about what actually worked |
| Aggressive salesperson | Scott's sales style is "value first, sale second" | Someone who earns the ask before making it |
| Academic / jargon-heavy | Both founders explain complex topics simply | A senior engineer explaining to a smart junior |
| Hype machine | "Magic AI" contradicts our "allergic to vaporware" stance | Grounded: "Intelligent automation", "practical AI", "systems that work" |
| Detached corporation | Both founders write in first person with emotion | "I", "We", "Our team" - always human |
