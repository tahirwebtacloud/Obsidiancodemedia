# Hook Category: Data & Stats (Hard Evidence)

**File Purpose:** Use when the post is anchored in research, experiments, or surprising metrics. The hook must make the data feel like a revelation — the number should feel shocking or counter-intuitive, not like a dry report. Specificity is critical: "I analyzed 1,000" beats "I analyzed many."

---

37. **The Mass Analysis**
Purpose: Does the heavy lifting of research so the reader doesn't have to.
Example: I analyzed 1,000 viral tweets. Here is what I found:
Template: I analyzed {large_number} {items}. Here is what I found:

38. **The Historical Data**
Purpose: Uses a long period of tracking to reveal an undeniable trend.
Example: 12 months of data reveals a surprising SEO insight:
Template: {Timeframe} of data reveals {surprising_insight}:

39. **The Undeniable Proof**
Purpose: Positions your following argument as an absolute fact.
Example: The numbers don't lie: video content is outperforming text.
Template: The numbers don't lie: {insight}

40. **The Micro-Lift**
Purpose: Connects a specific metric increase to a hyper-specific action.
Example: 45% lift from one tweak in our checkout flow:
Template: {n}% lift from one tweak in {topic}:

41. **The Experiment Results**
Purpose: Gamifies the data by showing the winners of a head-to-head test.
Example: 30 days, 15 landing page experiments — the winners:
Template: {n} days, {n2} experiments — the winners:

42. **The Statistics Hook**
Purpose: Surprising numbers grab attention and add credibility.
Example: Did you know that 70% of LinkedIn users never engage with posts?
Template: Did you know that {surprising metric}% of {target demographic} never {expected action}?
