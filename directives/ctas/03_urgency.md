# CTA Category: Creating Urgency (Direct Response)

**File Purpose:** Use when the post has a real deadline, limited spots, or a closing cart. The CTA must make missing out feel painful. Only use when the scarcity is genuine — fake urgency destroys trust and trains the audience to ignore future CTAs.

---

1. **The Simple Urgency**
Purpose: Clean, direct push to act before stock or spots run out.
Example: Don't miss out — selling fast. Click now!
Template: Don't miss out, selling fast, click-de-click!

2. **The Clock Ticker**
Purpose: Creates a sense that time is already running out.
Example: Hurry! Offer ends soon — grab yours before it's gone.
Template: Hurry! Offer ends soon!

3. **The Final Days Push**
Purpose: Signals the end is near without giving a precise time — keeps urgency high.
Example: Final days! Click the link now before this disappears.
Template: Final days! Swipe up now!

4. **The Sound of Scarcity**
Purpose: Uses humour and vivid language to make demand feel real and palpable.
Example: Hear that? That's the sound of them flying off the shelves — grab yours now!
Template: Swwossshhhhhh, that's the sound of them running out the door – grab yours now before they're all gone!

5. **The Limited Spots**
Purpose: Makes exclusivity real by putting a hard number on availability.
Example: Don't miss this — only 3 coaching spots left this month!
Template: Don't miss this! Only 3 spots left!

6. **The Free Trial Window**
Purpose: Lowers the barrier to commitment by framing it as a risk-free trial.
Example: Join for an entire month FREE — this week only. No risk, cancel anytime.
Template: Join for an entire month FREE, this week only.

7. **The No-Restock Warning**
Purpose: Eliminates the "I'll come back later" excuse by making clear there is no later.
Example: Get in quick — we're not restocking these.
Template: Get in quick, we're not re-stocking these bad boys.

8. **The Free Trial Claim**
Purpose: Combines urgency with zero commitment to eliminate hesitation.
Example: Claim your 7-day FREE trial now — sign up and get started today.
Template: Claim your 7 FREE day trial now, sign up to get started.

9. **The Pumpkin Deadline**
Purpose: Uses humour to make a midnight expiry feel fun rather than pressuring.
Example: Offer expires at midnight — get it before we turn into a pumpkin!
Template: Offer expires at midnight, get it before we turn into a pumpkin!

10. **The Upgrade Incentive**
Purpose: Combines a financial reward with a tight deadline to push annual upgrades.
Example: Upgrade and save — $50 off when you go annual. This week only!
Template: Upgrade and save, get $50 off when you buy annually (eep, this week only!)

11. **The Email List Urgency**
Purpose: Creates urgency around joining a list before a launch drops.
Example: You won't want to miss this — jump on the email list now. Dropping soon!
Template: You won't want to miss this! Jump on the email list now, dropping soon!
