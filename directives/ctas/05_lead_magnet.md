# CTA Category: Promoting a Lead Magnet

**File Purpose:** Use when the post directs the reader to a free resource — white paper, e-guide, webinar, course module, free challenge, or resource library. The CTA must make the free resource feel more valuable than what the reader just read. Best at the end of educational or authority posts that have already proven the quality of the knowledge on offer.

---

1. **The Scarcity Seat**
Purpose: Creates urgency by framing the free resource as limited or in-demand.
Example: Seats are filling up fast — grab your spot now before it's too late!
Template: Seats are limited, grab your spot now!

2. **The Free Module**
Purpose: Lowers commitment by letting the reader experience one piece first.
Example: Not sure? Try our first module completely FREE — no strings attached.
Template: Try our first module for FREE.

3. **The Netflix Binge**
Purpose: Reframes consuming content as entertainment rather than work — removes effort perception.
Example: Binge our FREE Masterclass before it gets taken off the internet.
Template: Binge our FREE Masterclass like it's being taken off Netflix.

4. **The Refresh Download**
Purpose: Positions the resource as a reset tool — good for quarterly or seasonal content.
Example: Ready to reset and refocus? Download the guide now and get your momentum back.
Template: Refresh and refocus for the next month, download now.

5. **The Grab-and-Go Template**
Purpose: Emphasises instant usability — the reader can start immediately.
Example: Grab your FREE template and start building today — no experience needed.
Template: Grab your FREE template and get started today.

6. **The DM-to-Receive**
Purpose: Drives DMs as a lead capture mechanism — high-intent signal from the reader.
Example: Want the full breakdown? DM us your email and we'll send it straight to you right now.
Template: Want the details? DM us your email address and we'll send it right now.

7. **The Comment-to-Unlock**
Purpose: Uses comment engagement to boost reach while gating the resource behind a micro-action.
Example: Comment "send it" and we'll DM you the webinar link instantly!
Template: Comment reserve my seat and we'll DM you the link to the webinar!

8. **The Proactive Guide**
Purpose: Flatters the reader's proactiveness and frames downloading as the smart move.
Example: Put your proactive hat on — download our free step-by-step e-book and get organised.
Template: Put your proactive pants on and get organised. Get started now by checking out our free e-book.

9. **The One-Click Promise**
Purpose: Minimises friction by making the path from post to resource feel tiny.
Example: You're literally one click away from solving this — head to the link in bio and we'll send you the free guide (valued at $97).
Template: Did you know, you're only one click away from _____. Head to link in bio and we'll send you a free ____ (valued at $__) showing you how.

10. **The DM Email Drop**
Purpose: Turns the comments section into a lead gen funnel — highly effective on LinkedIn.
Example: We'd love to send you our 10-step guide to growing on LinkedIn — DM us your email and it's in your inbox in minutes.
Template: Everything is figureoutable with a good ______. We'd love to send you our 10 step guide to ____ DM us your email and we'll send it straight to your inbox.
