# CTA Category: Promoting a Discount

**File Purpose:** Use when the post is built around a sale, promo code, or limited-time offer. The CTA must make the discount feel like a gift the reader would be foolish to miss. Inject humour or personality to lower sales resistance. Best at the end of product posts or promotional content.

---

1. **The Oprah CTA**
Purpose: Uses humour and a pop-culture reference to make the discount feel like a celebration.
Example: YOU get a discount, and YOU get a discount! Ok, we're not Oprah — but we can give you 30% off. DM us for yours!
Template: YOU get a car, and YOU get a car, and YOU get… ok, so maybe we're not Oprah, but we can give you 30% off, DM us for yours!

2. **The Tag-for-Code**
Purpose: Drives reach through tagging while rewarding the action with a discount.
Example: Tag 3 friends and we'll DM you a 30% off promo-code instantly!
Template: Tag 3 friends and we'll DM you a 30% off promo-code instantly!

3. **The Frictionless Discount**
Purpose: Removes every barrier between the reader and the purchase.
Example: 30% off — already applied at checkout. Simple. Shop now!
Template: 30% off? Yes, please! Applied at shopping cart, simples, shop now!

4. **The Midnight Deadline**
Purpose: Creates hard urgency by attaching the discount to a specific expiry point.
Example: The sun will come up tomorrow — this discount won't. Offer ends at midnight!
Template: The sun will come up tomorrow, but this discount won't! Offer ends midnight!

5. **The Guilt-Free Shopping CTA**
Purpose: Gives the reader permission to buy by reframing spending as a positive act.
Example: You're not shopping — you're helping the economy. Here's 25% off sitewide.
Template: You don't have a shopping problem. You're helping the economy. Need another reason to shop? Here's 25% off sitewide.

6. **The Surprise Gift**
Purpose: Adds unexpected delight to a purchase to tip the reader over the edge.
Example: Buy this week and we'll throw in gift wrap and a note that says "Congratulations, you won!" — it'll stay between us 😉
Template: Purchase something during our promotion this week and we'll add gift wrap and a note that says 'Congratulations, you won!'. It'll stay between us 😉

7. **The Secret Reader Discount**
Purpose: Rewards the reader for reading all the way to the end — creates a loyal insider feeling.
Example: Psst — because you've read this far, you get an extra 10% off. Use code _____ at checkout.
Template: Psst…because you've read this far down, you get a FURTHER 10% off! Use Promocode _____ at checkout.

8. **The In-Store Show-and-Save**
Purpose: Bridges social content to a physical store visit with a tangible reward.
Example: Come in, show us this post, and walk out with 30% off — simple.
Template: Come in store, show us this post and pocket 30% off in store.

9. **The Regret Anchor**
Purpose: Uses the fear of future regret to push the reader to act now.
Example: Nothing haunts us like the things we didn't buy on sale. Don't be haunted.
Template: Nothing haunts us like the things we didn't buy on sale. Get it girl.
