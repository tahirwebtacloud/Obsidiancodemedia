# LinkedIn Posting Guidelines

**Source:** [Miro Board Analysis](https://miro.com/app/board/uXjVGSePc6M=/?share_link_id=877113488387)

This document codifies the "LinkedIn Content Playbook" strategies for building a profitable personal brand.

## 1. Strategy: The Writing First Approach
The foundational goal is to become known for "one thing" by leveraging your unique market position.

### Core Concepts
- **Unfair Advantages**: Focus on what you know better than anyone else.
- **Monetizable Expertise**: Knowledge that directly changes someone’s outcome.
- **Strategic Arbitrage**: Taking what works in one industry (story, skill, knowledge) and applying it to another.

> **AI Tip**: Use prompts to identify your unfair advantages and strategic arbitrage topics based on your background to generate hooks.

## 2. Distribution: The Attention Framework
Success requires a "Distribution Engine" focused on the flow: **Attention → Retention → Conversion**.

### Types of Posts (The 3 Pillars)
1.  **High-Level Educational Content**
    *   *Purpose*: Pure value-based posts that teach the audience something specific.
    *   *Goal*: Establish competence and utility.
2.  **Elite Tier Storytelling**
    *   *Purpose*: Narrative-driven content focused on personal or client journeys.
    *   *Goal*: Build connection and relatability.
3.  **Authority Jacking**
    *   *Purpose*: Leveraging trends, news, or established authorities (e.g., "What I learned from X").
    *   *Goal*: Borrow credibility and ride existing waves of attention.

> **S-Tier Strategy**: Combine two of these types in a single post or across a content week for maximum impact.

---

### The "Distribution Engineering" Protocol (Reach = Comments)
*Based on analysis of Dec 2025 - Feb 2026 Data.*

**The Algorithm Truth:**
*   0 Comments = ~130 Reach (Dead Zone).
*   20+ Comments = ~1,200+ Reach (Viral Zone).
*   **Multiplier:** Every 1 comment adds ~50 quality impressions.

**The "Comment Bait" Checklist:**
1.  **The "Self-Reply"**: Immediately after posting, comment on your own post with a specific open question (e.g., "Curious - do you prioritize A or B?").
2.  **The "Friends & Family" DM**: Send the post link to 3-5 allies within 15 minutes. Ask: "Mind dropping a quick comment? It helps the algo."
4.  **The "Fan Club Tag"**: Identify your top 4-5 recurring reactors (The "Obsidian Fan Club"). Immediately after or within 1 hour of posting, tag one of them in your self-reply with a specific question (e.g., "@[Name], you worked on [Topic] recently—does this align with your experience?"). This anchors the algorithm instantly.
5.  **The "15 Reactor" Floor**: If your tactical content hits 15+ reactions, it is a success. Don't chase virality on niche posts; chase **Conversations Started**.

## 3. Content: Construction & Frameworks
Your ideas aren't boring; the way you write them is. Use these frameworks to ensure quality.

### The "Construct of a Post"
Follow this structural anatomy for high engagement:
1.  **Hook**: specific, 8-word limit.
2.  **Anchor**: Include a specific outcome or number early on.
3.  **Body**: Use varied sentence lengths to maintain rhythm (staccato vs. flowing).
4.  **Spacing**: Use "1 line - space - 1 line" or "1 line - space - 2 lines - space" for readability.
5.  **Value Delivery**: Provide either a direct step-by-step guide or a clear story-outcome.
6.  **Closing**: A clear "wrap-up" or TLDR style summary under 20 words.
7.  **CTA**: Prioritize a **Call to Engage** (comments/discussion) over a direct sales CTA.

### Writing Frameworks

#### The SLAY Framework
*   **S - Story**: Start with one line of a story. Use hooks like "I've..." or "How I...".
*   **L - Lesson**: State a quick key lesson without fluff.
*   **A - Actionable Advice**: Use listicles to show expertise and provide practical steps.
*   **Y - You**: Bring it back to the audience with a direct call to action or application.

#### The PAS Framework
*   **P - Problem**: Identify a painful problem (e.g., "Your LinkedIn content sucks").
*   **A - Agitate**: Highlight the cost of the problem (e.g., "It's costing you thousands").
*   **S - Solution**: Provide the fix (e.g., "Here is how to write content that sells").

### The Hook Strategy: 3 S's & 2 F's (Everyday CTA)
To cut through noise, ensure your hook hits one of these triggers:
1.  **Scary**: An industry-wiping trend or risk (e.g., "AI will kill manual data entry").
2.  **Strange**: Quirky, counter-intuitive, or "weird" take.
3.  **Sexy**: Highly desirable outcome (e.g., "How to audit your business in 30 mins").
4.  **Familiar**: Discussing a known entity (e.g., "What Warren Buffet would do...").
5.  **Free Value**: Giving away an expensive asset for $0 (e.g., "Steal my $5k SOP").

## 4. The 10-Step Operational Strategy
1.  Use **Storytelling** to reach new audiences fast.
2.  Provide **Tactical Content** to nurture them weekly.
3.  Use **Tactical CTAs** at the end of every post.
4.  Post **1-2 Lead Magnets** weekly to grow an email list.
5.  Send **1 Weekly Email** to that list to nurture further.
6.  Launch **1 New Offer** every quarter.
7.  Sell via **Daily Emails** for ~10 days during launches.
8.  Funnel warm buyers to a **Webinar** to 3x conversions.
9.  Build a **10/10 Product** and over-deliver.
10. Repeat.

## 5. Execution & Efficiency
- **Recipe File**: Maintain a collection of your most successful posts and favorite external posts to use as templates.
- **Repurposing**: Use AI to turn single high-performing posts into multiple formats.
- **Goal**: Manage the entire strategy in ~120 minutes of focused work once the system is built.
